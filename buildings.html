Built Environment

The artist Vasco Mourao illustrates abstract cityscapes. In response to my research of his work I decided to create my own abstract cityscapes by tessellating sections of buildings I had photographed. This created a kaleidoscope effect which I decided to recreate using lino this technique allowed me to accurately repeat the illustration.

The pieces above are a continuation of my lino print experimentations. Here I practised with low relief printing as a way of introducing colour into my prints. I think the use of lino within these pieces reflects the urban environment well as the messy look to the prints I similar to old, degraded street art or signs.

To the left are two research pages I created on brutalist architecture. I wanted to further investigate into buildings themselves to find out how they may reflect those who live there. This then prompted me to start incorporating portraiture into this project in order show how people interact with their environment.

The skull piece above was created in response to my research into the artists Christine Kim. I really liked how she combined tonal drawings with paper cut and so I decided to create a tonal drawing of a skull which I then cut gothic windows into. My decision to use a skull in this piece was based on how I think architecture has evolved. Intricate details within architecture are not as common now as people prefer a more modern look. Similar to that found in Brutalist architecture. In addition I created another kaleidoscope pattern out of a church window and then recreated the design using paper cut. I thought this was quite effective when layered on top of simpler shapes of blocked colour.

The final piece for this project is to the left. I Incorporated kaleidoscope patterns and I used a picture of a church window again to create this paper cut. I wanted to place this in the background of the piece, behind the head as a way of showing how this type of architecture is still standing and in the back of our minds. I then did a tonal study of a regular everyday building in my town that has a similar look to brutalist architecture. I placed this coming out of the eyes to show how nowadays people are surrounded by or “only have eyes for” this modern style of architecture which now surrounds us.

The top two sketchbook pages displayed above show my research pages into Pat Perry. As a result of my research I experimented with how I could combine tonal drawings of buildings with portraiture. The bottom two show my initial designs for my final piece which includes a brief look into the work of Mathew Borrett. These ideas I did reject as I wanted to included more realistic drawings within my final piece.
