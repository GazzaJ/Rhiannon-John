The Everyday

These are all biro and gouache studies that are part of a concertina I made during my fine art personal investigation. The idea of this piece was to create a visual diary documenting everyday objects which I used over the course of a week. During this project I have been investigating how still life has evolved to reflect modern life and so by documenting objects used by someone I think you can almost create a portrait.

To finish these studies I decided to collage receipts, black card and shopping bags around the study. In some areas this was simply for decoration but for others I incorporated these aspects into the study. For example I used a Waterstones carrier bag to create the shadow of a nail polish bottle. In addition by using similar colours and patterns throughout the concertina I created a continuing theme which linked all the studies together.

The following images are taken from a still life photoshoot I did in response to my research on Vanitas. This genre of still life uses various objects that only wealthy people would have owned in order to symbolise wealth and power. I took inspiration from this aspect of vanitas and created a still life composition consisting of branded everyday objects. These objects could hold a similar weight to those found in early vanitas pieces as due to the cost of living crisis they may also only be owned by wealthier people as they are the more expensive versions of products available.

After I took the photos I edited a select few using photoshop. I used tools like dodge and burn in order darken and lighten areas. By pairing this with the curves and photo filter tools found I was able to add shadows and tints to the photos. This created a dark, brooding atmosphere similar to vanitas paintings.

To continue developing my work based around the cost of living crisis I created a biro study on a receipt. I then photocopied this study and experimented with different ways of incorporating embroidery and other surfaces into the drawing. I turned the study shown in the bottom left hand corner into a relief piece. This helped to add dimension to the piece as beforehand the objects appeared merged together due to the blue biro.

These two pieces are further experiments I did when figuring out how to combine embroidery with biro drawings. I have used receipts throughout this project as it has allowed me to include physical versions of everyday objects in my work which in turn makes them more personal. To create this experiment I used procreate to layer an embroidered receipt over a biro study of a section of Audrey Flack's “Wheel of fortune” painting.
