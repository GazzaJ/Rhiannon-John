Art Textiles

Above are samples of beauty product labels I designed as a way of expanding on my ideas relating to issues surrounding body image. For example, the label titled “Baby Soft” refers to a moisturising product which to link with the name I have planned to have a sickly sweet pastel pink colour pallet and possibly a ruffled lace lining. I then created some samples using appliqué and by heat pressing plastic and fabric scraps.

The samples shown above were created using techniques such as wet felting, needle felting, weaving, crochet and shibori. I sketched out the initial designs for the woven piece, however for the felted piece (above) I recorded my initial designs by collaging coloured paper and photocopies of older samples. This is a technique I have started to use more frequently when recording ideas as it allows me to map out any colours and textures I may want to include in a sample.

For these samples I mainly focused on man-made structures as I thought the sharp edges and shapes found within modern architecture could be conveyed well via the use of screen printing. However, during my research I also found images of Turkey tail mushrooms which I then turned into a possible design that I could transfer using sublimation paper and a heat press. I added this natural form to my other prints to create contrast as the organic shapes here are the complete opposite to those found in man-made structures.

To the left is two pages from my Y12 textiles sketchbook where I recorded photos and sketches of primarily man-made architecture (The highlighted images are not mine). I then used the shaped within these photos to create templates for screen printing. I then made multiple prints where I experimented with different compositions by layers the individual templates in top of one another.

The images below are some more examples of my screen and sublimation prints. I enhanced these prints by continuing sections with embroidery and by sewing on the stencils used for the original printing which worked quite well as it added more dimension to the piece.

Below are a collection of photos I took and edited during a textiles unit title “Structure”. When taking these photos I was looking for any interesting shapes and textures within natural and man-made structures. I then converted the images to black and white as I didn’t want any colour within the piece to distract from the various textures I was planning on recreating.

Below and left are thumbnail sketches I drew in response to the images I in the section above. I tried to capture the different texture in the photos by using watercolour, fine liner and various forms of mark making. I then tried to recreate the marks from my sketches with textiles techniques like embroidery, applique and needle felting; images of which are below and to the right.

Later on in the “Structure” unit I created more sketches that I would go on to use as a reference when using a heat press to create surfaces with recycled plastic bags. The image below and left is a collection of up-close images of cells which I found on the internet. I then used these as a reference to create thumbnails , below and right, out of watercolour, coloured pencil and magazine pages.
